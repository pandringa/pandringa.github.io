About

Hello! My name is Peter Andringa, and I'm currently a high school student at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology, an elite high school in Northern Virginia specifically for students who love math, science, engineering, and computer science. I'm currently in my Sophomore year, and even though its a lot of work I really enjoy studying there.

My first introduction to computer science came in 7th grade, when I picked up a cousin's C++ book and started to read it. I thought the idea of programming was fascinating, but I didn't quite understand everything going on in that book. I decided to teach myself how to code, by building a hands on project: creating an iPhone app. I started to teach myself, and eventually finished my app, a game called "Sharks Vs. Subs", in 8th grade. (I've since been removed it from the App Store, but you can check it out on The Wayback Machine).

Over the last couple years since then I've developed a passion for entrepreneurship, product design, and making my ideas come to life through code. Outside of school I spend much of my time devoted to these things, whether it be through internships, helping lead my school's entrepreneurship club, organizing HackTJ and PilotDC, or even just tinkering on some of my own projects. I love building cool stuff and teaching others how to do so, because I think there's nothing cooler than seeing your idea come to life.

I'm always open to new opportunities or ideas, so don't hesitate to contact me via email (peter@andrin.ga), because I'd love to hear from you!

Projects

Light Sensor

This project, built for a school science experiment measuring the feeding rate of oysters, uses a Raspberry Pi and Adafruit's lumosity sensor to measure and record the amount of light on the sensor. The data is then logged and uploaded to a server for analysis.

View on GitHub

Light Notifier

A system of LED notifications using a Raspberry Pi, which connects to various web services (e.g. Facebook, Twitter, GitHub, Google+) to check for unread notifications. The notifier is configurable through a Node.js web portal available on the local network.

Coming soon to GitHub

Chat Embedder

A Safari and Chrome extension that will automatically embed the content from any valid link (to Youtube, Vimeo, SoundCloud, etc.), directly into your Facebook chat. Powered by the embed.ly API, allowing the user to embed content from over 250 websites.
